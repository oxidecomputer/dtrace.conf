theshteves 12/11/2024 9:53 AM
I'm a quiet independent researcher in Indiana obsessed with FPGA's/reconfigurable architectures, not as stepping stones for developing ASICs but rather as THE post-CPU target for fundamentally simpler, more legible, observable, easier-to-reprogram SoC's in the future. More precisely, I want to help build out more usable compiler infrastructure for synthesizing hardware accelerators from high-level software. I'm not at all interested in AI/ML, but rather generating the long-tail of one-off boutique "just speed up my hot function" digital circuits that no hardware engineer could ever justify the time to artisanally hand-craft. We need better, leaner primitives to help software engineers better reason about hardware, beyond what they learned about CPUs in their OS class. I don't have slides to present, but am happy to discuss & even just gather feedback from others. I love Oxide's similar focus on rethinking every layer of computing to reduce complexity & increase the throughput of r&d! The same way we went from analog->digital & CISC->RISC (over-generalizing here), we're sorely in need of "subtractive" innovations that make it easier to onboaard the next generation of 2030's jr. engineers (edited)
